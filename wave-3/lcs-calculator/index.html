You’ve smoked pack-years.

Here’s what that means.

Pack-years are a way to estimate a smoker’s tobacco exposure over a lifetime. A pack-year is the equivalent of smoking 20 cigarettes per day for a year. So if you smoked a pack a day for 30 years, that equals 30 pack-years of smoking. Smoking 2 packs a day for 20 years equals 40 pack-years, and so on.

This measurement is used by doctors to help determine if a person is at risk of developing lung cancer. Other factors aside, people with at least a 30 pack-year history of smoking and who are between the ages of 55 and 80 are at high risk. This makes them candidates for lung cancer screening.

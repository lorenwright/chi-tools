what are the signs I might have diabetes?

The symptoms for diabetes can be hard to detect. Many people only learn they have the disease after it has caused long-term damage.

Roll overSwipe the figure to learn about some of the telltale signs of diabetes.

Fatigue

When your blood sugar levels are out of balance, cells don’t operate efficiently, robbing you of energy and leaving you feeling tired.

Blurred vision

Diabetes makes it difficult for your body to maintain consistent fluid levels. This can affect the ability of your eyes to stay focused.

Increased thirst

Being thirsty all the time is a sign that your body is struggling to stay properly hydrated as your kidneys work overtime.

Increased hunger

Without enough insulin in your system, the food you eat isn’t properly converted into energy, so your body craves more.

Increased urination

When your blood sugar levels are high, your kidneys have a hard time reabsorbing it all. So the kidneys expel it by making more urine.

Schedule an appointment

Catching diabetes early can prevent it from developing into a major, life-threatening problem. Talk to your health care provider about a simple blood sugar screening to check your levels.

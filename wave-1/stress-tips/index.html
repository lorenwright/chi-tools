• 5

    stress relief tips for cancer patients

    Life was stressful enough before you got cancer. But try to relax - these 5 tips will help you cope.

  • 1 Exercise regularly

    Take a walk, ride a bike, do some stretching or yoga. Exercise helps the brain release chemicals that make you feel good. Start slow and keep sessions short. If possible, include weights and muscle-building exercises a few times a week. Over time, try to reach 30 minutes of moderate to vigorous activity a day.

  • 2 Practice deep breathing

    Sit down, relax and fill your lungs with air. Deep breathing slows the heart rate and lowers blood pressure. Sit up straight, close your eyes and slowly inhale deeply through your nose. Finish by exhaling through your mouth. Repeat for 5 minutes for a calmer you.

  • 3 Listen to music

    Cue the smooth jazz. Or whatever music you find soothing. It can even be a playlist of birds chirping in a meadow or waves lapping the beach. Let your mind focus on the melodies or sounds and take you to a peaceful, happy place.

  • 4 Learn a new hobby

    Our minds and bodies welcome distractions from our daily routines. Taking part in new activities challenges your thinking and gives you a sense of accomplishment. So learn calligraphy, take a photography class or start a blog.

  • 5 Keep a journal

    Writing in a journal lets you express your feelings in a private place. You may feel a sense of relief when you get something off your chest without having to worry about how your friends or family might react.

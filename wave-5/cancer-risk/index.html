are you at risk for cancer?

There are more than 100 types of cancer. Some cancers are caused by things beyond your control. But there are things you can do to reduce your risk of being exposed to cancer causing agents. Simply taking this quiz may be one of them.

where do you face the greatest cancer risk?

in your garden or your basement

Either answer can be correct. In your garden, you face exposure to the sun and skin cancer risk. So use sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher) to protect against harmful rays.

In some locations, basements are a collection point for radon gases that occur naturally underground. Radon is a cancer-causing radioactive gas that you can’t see or smell. An inexpensive test kit can be used to see if your basement has high levels of radon.

correct answer: at your job

Let’s assume you’re not a mail carrier or personal trainer. For most people, work is fairly sedentary. There’s recent evidence that prolonged sitting increases the risk of developing some types of cancer. So if you have a desk job, consider a standing desk. Or visit the mall over your lunch hour – the walking will do you good.

correct answer: at a backyard barbeque

While a big hunk of grilled red meat has its appeal, a small serving of free range organic quail may present a smaller cancer risk. Research suggests that a diet rich in red meat and processed meats like hot dogs and sausages presents a greater risk for certain cancers than a plant-based diet.

correct answer: drinking water from a garden hose

Water can flush toxins out of your system. But drinking from an outdoor hose can expose you to chemicals used to manufacture plastics. Even rubber hoses can contain pathogens. On the other hand, milk is rich in calcium, which can reduce the development of colon polyps. So reach for the milk, but please…use a glass. And don’t forget to keep hydrated with 8 – 10 glasses of water every day.
